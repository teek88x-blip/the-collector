Session 02

The Better Offer

The letter sat overnight. That alone says something.

The party split to learn what they could. Croakelion sought counsel at the church of Savus and received a vision, danger ahead, but something worth the cost. Mai-amar went to his adoptive mother Wilson, but she knew nothing beyond what Cliff had already shared. Sticky and Bannanas returned to the tavern and met Trevor, whose friend Jose had taken work from the Collector. Good money. Real danger.

The Chandler's shop led them upstairs to a rented room, two chairs, a large mirror, and nothing else. Robyn the chandler had never met her tenant. Cash upfront, a key left for whoever came asking. When they settled in, the mirror did the rest. The Collector spoke, terms were offered, and a contract to Ironpeak Fortress was accepted.

On the road the next day, bandits tried an ambush. Croakelion caught it at the last second. Bannanas stepped up to meet the leader while the rest folded in behind. It was over quickly, the kind of quickly that makes a point.

Your character just accepted dangerous work from a voice in a mirror. What's the biggest risk they've taken on someone else's word before, and would they do it again?

Session 01

The Cliff and the Letter

Four hunters took a cliff face for a promise of coin and the head of a mountain troll. Not the worst reason to risk a fall.

The cave at the summit offered its own welcome, a den of Mawbs, feral and busy with a horse that had seen better days. Sticky and Croakelion stepped inside to investigate while Mai-amar held position at the mouth. A sensible enough arrangement, until the troll made its own introduction from behind.

What followed split into two fights the party may never fully reconcile. Sticky rushed to Mai-amar's side, and together the two of them brought the creature down, no small thing for a goblin and an elf against a mountain troll. Meanwhile, Croakelion conducted his own war underground, carving through Mawbs with a thoroughness that left remarkably little evidence. A fight no one else saw and few traces to prove it happened.

Battered, they made their way back toward Carrow. The road offered one oddity, a courier, old and unhurried, with no particular explanation for his presence. Then the familiar warmth of Cliff's tavern, payment for the job, and a name passed along: someone called the Tax Collector, looking for reliable hands.

The evening ended at Croakelion's hovel, generously self-titled a "mansion," beside Miss Mabel's, where a letter was waiting. Addressed to each of them. By name.

Your party just took a real beating for modest pay from a man they trust. Now a stranger who already knows their names is offering work. What is it about your character's life right now that makes them consider saying yes instead of walking away?

Session Zero

The Professionals

Four hunters. Not chosen. Not destined. Simply good enough at dangerous work to still be alive, and smart enough to know that matters. They have bled together, argued over coin, shared bad food on worse roads, and learned when to trust each other's instincts without asking why. They are not friends by any simple definition. They are something more useful than that.

Croakelion
Kroaken Seraph. A towering amphibian figure who carries himself with the absolute conviction of divinity. He believes he is the sole Kroaken in existence, and his golden wings of light do little to discourage the assumption. Whether the gods agree with his self-appointment is a question he has not yet thought to ask.
Sticky
Goblin Wizard. Small, sharp, and half-trained by a forgetful mentor named Old Jack. What Sticky lacks in formal education he makes up for in the kind of reckless ingenuity that keeps a goblin alive long enough to become dangerous. The magic works. The theory behind it is another matter entirely.
Mai-amar
Elf Rogue. Raised by a circus troupe, orphaned by violence, taken in by those who chose to care. He moves like a performer and fights like someone who learned early that grace and survival are the same skill. There is a warmth to him that the work has not yet managed to reach.
Bannanas
Simiah Warrior. A natural climber with a prehensile tail and an approach to bounty hunting best described as enthusiastic. Quick hands, quicker feet, and the kind of confidence that comes from knowing there is always something higher to climb to.

Every hunter has a history before the hunt. The questions below are for those who wish to set theirs down in writing.
